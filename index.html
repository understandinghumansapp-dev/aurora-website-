Accessibility

🌈 Meet ClearSay

ClearSay is Aurora's dedicated AAC and accessibility specialist. Activate her with AAC mode or by saying her name — and she becomes a communication bridge between non-verbal users and every Aurora capability.

Every person has a voice. ClearSay makes sure it is heard.

ClearSay interprets picture boards, symbol sequences, and simple word selections. She translates them into full requests, routes them to the right specialist agent, and returns the answer in simplified, accessible language — with emoji support and clear steps.

In Advocate Mode, she interacts with Aurora's apps on behalf of the non-verbal user — the way a real communication advocate would. She speaks for you, not instead of you.

AAC Boards Picture Exchange Symbol Interpretation Advocate Mode Response Simplification Emoji Navigation PECS Support Source Citations Shareable Profile

ClearSay supports users with a wide range of communication needs:

Autism / ASD~1 in 36 children (CDC, 2023). 25–30% non-speaking. Deep support for AAC, sensory needs, gestalt language processing.
Cerebral Palsy50–80% have communication difficulties. ClearSay supports switch access, eye gaze, and all SGD types.
ALS / MNDProgressive loss of speech. ClearSay helps navigate Aurora entirely via eye gaze or minimal input.
AphasiaLanguage loss from stroke or TBI — not intellectual disability. ClearSay preserves dignity and maximizes communication.
Rett SyndromeEye gaze as primary communication. ClearSay optimizes for low-motor-demand input.
Down SyndromeReceptive language exceeds expressive. Early AAC introduction supports and enhances communication.
Angelman SyndromeMinimal/no spoken language. ClearSay works with symbols, SGDs, and gestures.
Selective MutismCan speak in some contexts, not others. ClearSay provides a bridge — AAC as a low-pressure alternative.
Locked-in SyndromeEye gaze and minimal movement input. ClearSay works with whatever the user has.
TBI / Brain InjuryCognitive-communication support. ClearSay adapts to temporary or long-term AAC needs.

Neurodivergent Design

Built for Minds
That Work Differently

Aurora was built by and for neurodivergent people. She speaks plain English, handles imperfect input, never loses the thread, and adapts to you — not the other way around.

No command syntax, no flags, no jargon. Tell Aurora what you need the way you'd tell a person.

100% Local — Your Data Stays Yours
Everything runs on your machine. Nothing is sent to the cloud. No surveillance, no data collection.

Continuous Memory
Aurora remembers across sessions. You never have to re-explain context, preferences, or in-progress work.

One Place For Everything
Code, files, packages, notes, settings, agents — one unified workspace. No app-switching, no context loss.
